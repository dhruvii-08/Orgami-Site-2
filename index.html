Interesting Facts About Camels:

  • Camels have thick lips, allowing them to hunt for prickly vegetation that other animals cannot chew.
  • To keep sand out of their eyes, camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes.
  • Camel breeds are divided into two types: one-humped or "dromedary" camels and two-humped Bactrian camels.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons:

  • Pigeons are extremely sophisticated and clever creatures.
  • Pigeons are well-known for their exceptional navigation skills.
  • Pigeons are extremely gregarious creatures.

Interesting Facts About Chameleons:

  • Their feet are like salad tongs.
  • Almost half of the known species are in Madagascar.
  • Skin crystals allow them to change color at any time.

Interesting Facts About Teddies:

  • The term "bear embrace" was coined in 1846.
  • The phrase "teddy bear" was first used in 1906, according to the Oxford English Dictionary.
  • The Teddy Bear Two Step was the original title of The Teddy Bears' Picnic tune.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas:

  • Cicada nymphs, or babies, can survive a massive fall.
  • The majority of them have red-orange eyes.
  • It is not uncommon for bugs to have a short adult life.
